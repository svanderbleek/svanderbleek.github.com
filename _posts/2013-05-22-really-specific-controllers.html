--- layout: post title: Really Specific Controllers ---

As a web developer beautiful resource routes are important, especially when using Rails which makes it very easy to think resourcefully. An early principle I learned was not to nest resources deeper than two levels. I've refined this position in my own use to follow a pattern of deffering sub-resource management to a specific sub-resource controller but allowing creation namespaced by the creating resource, which acts like a context for filling in the sub-resources parameters.

So Use

resource/sub-resource/new

for a creation form then post to

sub-resource

for actual database creation and continue with

sub-resource/:id

for manipulation.
